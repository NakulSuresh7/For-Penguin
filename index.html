the memory i carry

The beach. The pool. You.

You showed up multiple times that day. Nobody asked you to. You just did — because that is who you are. That is the kind of love most people spend their whole lives looking for, and somehow I get to have it.

And then there was the pool. The two of us holding each other in the water, letting ourselves cry — for my dad, for everything we were carrying. You weren't afraid of my grief. You didn't look away. You stepped right into it with me.

I think about that moment often. How heavy it was, and how much lighter it felt because you were there.

in memory, with love

For my Dad — and for you

He wasn't here long enough. And yet, in some quiet way, he mattered to you too — even without many shared moments. The fact that you felt his loss with me, that you held space for someone you never truly got to know, tells me everything about who you are.

As his death anniversary approaches, and my birthday follows just two days later — I feel both the weight of missing him and the warmth of having you. You have made the hard days survivable and the ordinary days beautiful.

I believe he would have loved you. Loved you the way I do — completely, and without question.
